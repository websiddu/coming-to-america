• Resume for On Campus job

    Irrespective of what your background, you have to prepare a one page resume. It can be with the following detatils,

    • Just listundergraduate GPA and list some of your projects done in Bachelors.
    • If you have worked, list it under work experience. Try to highlight your student involvement.
    • Most of the jobs on campus focus on good customer skills. Focus your resume on highlighting your customer experience. One good way is, You can say, I was part of an Organization as vice president or treasurer.
    • If you have any awards or accomplishments like Top in class, college, highlight them. Scholarships, etc. Also, if you have done anything in society like red cross or blood donation, focus on them.

    Overall, the goal should be to highlight your people skills. If you do not have any now, try to get involved in community now so that you can put in resume when you come up here. You may not need a resume in some situations like if you get by reference. But, it is good to have one.

  • Find the job postings

    One of the regular ways is to go to Career center on campus and look for the on campus postings. There will be a careers website and they will be posted there too depending on the school. Guess what? Everyone knows this and there is high competition.

    A very creative way to get on campus job is, join a bunch of student organizations and network with people. Ask them where they work on campus and get networked. It is quick and effective. You will have to be committed to student orgs. Do not join to get job. Join to get to know people and be involved. Job will follow you based on your work.

    Another proven way is to sign up for any volunteering opportunity in Student life office or any office that is in Student Union. If you volunteer, you get to know the bosses of the offices. You can ask them if there are any openings. They see you volunteering and guess what, you get a job next day. Some good places are International Student office where we volunteer a lot.

  • On the day of the interview

    First impressions really do count. If you get an interview you can assume that your potential employers already like what they have seen. The interview is an opportunity for you to build on that impression to secure the job.

    Good preparation is the key to staying in control. Plan your route, allowing extra time for any unexpected delays, and get everything you need to take with you ready the night before. Remember to speak clearly, smile and remember that your interviewers are just normal people, and they may be nervous too!

    Tips
    • Map and directions of how to get to the interview
    • The interview invitation letter, a copy of your Curriculum Vitae (CV), cover letter and your application form (if you sent one)
    • Contact details of your references
    • List of questions to ask about the job or company
    • Pen and paper to make notes and show you are prepared
    • Cash change if you need to get a taxi at the last minute or some other unforeseen expenditure
